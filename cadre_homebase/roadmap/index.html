Course details by phase

Hover or click a course to see description.

Pre-reqs

Foundations and Technical Skills

Focus: Core visual design, digital media literacy, and technical skill-building.

ART 13 – 2D Foundations (Adobe Creative Suite)
Introductory design fundamentals using Photoshop, Illustrator, etc.; principles of composition, color, and digital layout.
ART 74/75 – Foundations & Intro to Digital Media Art
Basics of digital image and vector creation, HTML/CSS, and web media integration.

Lower Division

Web and Interactive Media

Focus: Coding and creative technology applied to digital art, web, and interactive projects.

ART 101 – Creative Coding
Introduction to coding for artists—HTML/CSS and JavaScript for creative web applications.

3D, Animation, and Immersive Media

Focus: Digital 3D environments, animation, VFX, extended reality, and fabrication.

ART 102 – Experimental 3D
Exploration of 3D modeling, animation, VFX, and 3D printing for creative projects.

Video, Audio, and Systems-Based Media

Focus: Time-based and systems-oriented media combining sound, interactivity, and complex signal processes.

ART 103 – Art as System
Visual and audio programming, signal processing, and sound synthesis—art informed by computational systems.
ART 105 – Advanced Digital Video
High-level video production with projection mapping and installation contexts.

Theory and Criticism

Focus: Conceptual frameworks, media history, and theoretical discussions on technology and art.

ART 104 – Contemporary Media Theory
Examination of relationships between art, science, and technology; current debates in media theory.
ARTH 110 – History and Theory of New Media
Historical survey of media art and technology's influence on artistic practice.

Upper Division

Web and Interactive Media

ART 109 – Advanced Internet Technology
Advanced front-end and creative coding with Node.js and Three.js, emphasizing interactive and 3D web experiences.

3D, Animation, and Immersive Media

ART 107 – Advanced Projects in Media Art
World-building and 3D environment design, likely integrating digital sculpture and interactive space design.
ART 175 – Advanced Projects (x3D & XR)
Advanced web-based 3D and XR (AR/MR/VR) production.

Human–Machine Interaction and Physical Computing

Focus: Hardware, sensors, and interactive interfaces bridging physical and digital artmaking.

ART 106 – Topics in Human–Machine Interface
Physical computing, CAD/CAM, and fabrication methods for interactive works.

Game Design and Interactive Narrative

Focus: Games as an art form and creative medium.

ART 108 – Game Design
Study and creation of games, with emphasis on conceptual development and production using game engines.

Capstone

Theory and Criticism

ART 210 – Graduate Seminar
Advanced critique and theory discussion integrating critical writing, art theory, and professional review.

Professional Practice and Exhibition

Focus: Preparation for professional art careers, curatorial practice, and exhibition organization.

ART 198 – Professional Practice
Building professional skills through show planning, documentation, and industry preparation.
ART 195 – BFA Group Show
Collaborative exhibition planning, installation, and professional presentation.

Sample four-year roadmap

From the official catalog. Consult MyPlanner and your major advisor. Students need 60+ units to enroll in GE UD courses. *Courses marked with an asterisk may be taken in a different semester.

Year Fall Spring

  • GE Area 1A 3 units
  • GE Area 1C 3 units*
  • GE Area 2 3 units*
  • One from: ART 44HA, DSGN 44HA, ENGL 44HA, HUM 44HA, OR PHIL 44HA – Design Your Life… 3 units
 Spring (15–16 units)
  • ART 2 – The Artist in Contemporary Culture 3 units
  • ART 74 – Introduction to Digital Media 3 units
  • GE Area 1B 3 units

  • GE Upper Division Area 4 3 units*

  • Studio Elective 3 units

Roadmap notes

  • * Courses marked with an asterisk may be taken in a different semester than listed.
  • Lower division courses should generally be taken in years 1–2, and upper division courses in years 3–4.
  • Work with an academic advisor to create an individual roadmap using the MyPlanner tool.
  • A minimum of 120 units is required for this degree.
